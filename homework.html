A right triangle is a triangle in which one angle is a right angle (that is, a 90-degree angle). The relation between the sides and angles of a right triangle is the basis for trigonometry.

Like any polygon, the perimeter is the total distance around the outside, which can be found by adding together the length of each side. Or as a formula:

Perimeter = a + b + c where: a,b and c are the lengths of each side of the triangle
